# Garden
Garden Plan 2026
A simple month-by-month garden plan for Ireland.

## Assumptions

- Location: Midlands, Ireland
- Start point: late April 2026
- Style: beginner-friendly, small garden, pots or one raised bed
- Climate note: inland frost risk can linger into late spring, so tender crops are safer outdoors from mid- to late May onward

## Best beginner crops for Midlands

Start with crops that are forgiving and do well in Irish conditions:

- Potatoes
- Lettuce and salad leaves
- Swiss chard
- Scallions
- Peas
- Broad beans
- Parsley
- Chives
- Thyme
- One courgette plant after frost risk eases

## Simple starter setup

A good first setup:

- 1 raised bed for potatoes and greens
- 3 to 5 pots for herbs and salad leaves
- 1 large container for a courgette in late May
- Compost
- Hand trowel
- Watering can
- Fleece for cold nights

## Late April 2026

### Jobs

- Plant seed potatoes
- Sow lettuce, salad leaves, spinach or chard, peas, broad beans, and scallions
- Start courgettes, pumpkins, basil, and tomatoes indoors or under cover
- Weed beds and containers
- Add compost or well-rotted manure to planting areas

### Focus

This is the setup month. Stick to hardy crops outdoors and hold tender crops back.

## May

### Early May

- Keep sowing lettuce, peas, chard, beetroot, and carrots
- Earth up potatoes as they grow
- Thin seedlings so they have room
- Keep weeds down

### Mid to late May

- Harden off indoor-raised plants for about a week
- Plant out courgettes, pumpkins, French beans, and outdoor tomatoes once nights are mild
- Put herbs into pots near the house
- Keep fleece ready in case of a cold snap

### Focus

This is the main planting month. Most of the garden gets moving now.

## June

### Jobs

- Succession-sow lettuce and salad leaves every 2 to 3 weeks
- Sow more beetroot, scallions, and chard
- Plant any last courgettes or beans
- Water properly during dry spells
- Mulch around plants to hold moisture and reduce weeds

### Focus

Keep things steady. Small repeat sowings are better than planting everything at once.

## July

### Jobs

- Harvest new potatoes, lettuce, peas, and herbs
- Pick courgettes while they are still small
- Sow another round of salad leaves, chard, and scallions
- Remove tired or bolting plants
- Reuse empty space for fresh sowings

### Focus

Harvest often and refill gaps as crops finish.

## August

### Jobs

- Keep harvesting potatoes, courgettes, herbs, and greens
- Sow spinach, oriental leaves, or winter salad leaves in a sheltered spot
- Clear finished plants quickly
- Watch for disease on older plants
- Start noting what worked and what did not

### Focus

This is the bridge into autumn growing.

## September

### Jobs

- Harvest maincrop potatoes if you planted them
- Keep cutting chard and herbs
- Protect crops on cold nights with fleece if needed
- Compost healthy plant waste
- Add compost to empty beds

### Focus

Growth slows, nights cool down, and protection starts to matter again.

## October

### Jobs

- Finish lifting potatoes before persistent wet weather or frost
- Harvest remaining chard, herbs, and late salads
- Remove old tomato and courgette plants
- Mulch empty beds with compost or manure
- Plant garlic for an easy overwinter crop if you want one

### Focus

Clear, tidy, and improve the soil for next year.

## November

### Jobs

- Harvest any remaining hardy greens
- Protect herbs or salads in pots
- Clean tools, trays, and supports
- Order seeds and seed potatoes for next year
- Write down what performed best

### Focus

Keep maintenance light and get organized for spring.

## December

### Jobs

- Keep beds weed-free
- Top up the compost heap
- Sketch next year’s layout
- Decide where potatoes and courgettes will go next year
- Buy compost, pots, and fleece before the spring rush

### Focus

Rest, plan, and make next season easier.

## Basic growing rules

### Sun

Choose the sunniest spot you have, ideally with 6 or more hours of sunlight.

### Soil

Add compost before planting. Good soil solves many problems.

### Water

Water the base of plants, not the leaves. Water deeply, not constantly.

### Frost

Do not rush tender crops outdoors. Keep fleece ready for cold nights.

### Size

Start small. One well-managed bed beats a large neglected one.

## Easiest first-year layout

### Raised bed

- Potatoes at one end
- Lettuce, chard, and scallions at the other end

### Pots

- Parsley
- Chives
- Thyme
- Mint in its own pot

### Large container

- One courgette from late May

## Quick monthly checklist

```text
APR: potatoes, peas, beans, salads, compost, prep
MAY: main sowing, harden off, plant tender crops late in month
JUN: repeat sowing, mulch, water, weed
JUL: harvest and refill gaps
AUG: keep harvesting, sow autumn leaves
SEP: protect from cold nights, compost empty beds
OCT: clear old plants, lift potatoes, mulch beds
NOV: tidy, clean, order seeds
DEC: plan next year
```

## Recommended first-year priority order

If you want to keep it very simple, do this in order:

1. Potatoes
2. Lettuce or salad leaves
3. Chard
4. Herbs in pots
5. Peas
6. One courgette

## Notes

- Mint spreads fast, so keep it in its own pot
- Courgettes need a lot of room and regular picking
- Potatoes are great for beginners because they are rewarding and easy to follow
- Salad leaves give quick wins and help build confidence
